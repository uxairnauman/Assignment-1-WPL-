Introduction

Story of the Site

Each page of this portfolio captures a different piece of my CSC 337 journey at Iqra University Islamabad: personal introduction, day-to-day hobbies, media inspirations, and a full contact roster for viva coordination.

How It Is Built

The entire project uses semantic HTML only. Headers, sections, and articles break up the narrative so every requirement—navigation, tables, lists, media embeds—remains easy to follow without CSS.

What You Can Explore

Daily Habits & Games

The My Hobbies page turns my weekly routine into an ordered loop, lists the creative breaks I take, and explains why Valorant and Badminton balance digital and physical play. Two tables compare hobbies and favorite games between me and a close friend.

Media Inspirations

The My Favorite Media page uses tables for layout: social links and both local and online images appear on the left, while five embedded YouTube videos on the right form a study playlist I rotate through.

Contact & Campus Details

The Contact page lists my designation, email, phone, and every group member ID. It also highlights the Iqra University Islamabad campus address and embeds a live Google Map so mentors can locate our usual meeting spot.

Navigation Flow

The top navigation mirrors the reading order: start here on Home for context, review hobbies to understand my routines, dive into the media gallery to see current influences, and end on Contact for logistics. Internal links ensure you can jump among pages without losing track.

Why It Matters

Practice With Semantics

By structuring every description inside <section> and <article> tags, the site shows how semantic HTML alone can deliver a cohesive narrative.

Preparation for Viva

Because each paragraph points to real content, I can quickly explain the intent of every page during viva sessions—no guessing about what lives where.

Next Steps

I will continue updating the hobby tables, media embeds, and contact schedule as semester projects evolve. Keep an eye on the navigation bar for newly linked work once the course progresses.
